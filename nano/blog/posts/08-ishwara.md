Ishwara is another name for what the Upanishads call Brahman. The claim is that this is the only thing that exists.

Every form, every event, every distance between objects is Ishwara appearing as appearance. The tree is Ishwara. The thought about the tree is Ishwara. The one having the thought is Ishwara.

What makes this hard to hold is that ordinary experience presents exactly the opposite picture. There are many things. They are separate. Some are near and some are far. This is the structure of experience, which Vedanta calls maya. Not illusion in the sense of fake, but appearance in the sense of partial.

The full statement is Aham Brahmasmi: I am Brahman. Not connected to Brahman, not a part of Brahman. The distance between the individual and the total is itself an appearance within the total. There is no one standing outside Ishwara looking in. The looker is also that.

Most of the Vedic literature is spent making this stick, not as an idea held loosely, but as something actually seen.
